⚖️
Your child has federal rights. IDEA & Section 504 protect them.
Under IDEA, schools must provide a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in the Least Restrictive Environment (LRE). Under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, any student with a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits a major life activity is entitled to accommodations — regardless of academic performance. You do not need to prove your child is failing to qualify.
📋
Which plan are you preparing for?
Not sure which applies? A 504 is easier to get and covers most medically complex kids. An IEP provides more intensive services but requires an educational disability category.
504
Section 504 Plan
Accommodations to level the playing field. Covers any physical or mental condition that limits a major life activity — including chronic illness, fatigue, pain, anxiety, ADHD, and more.
✦ Best for: medical complexity, chronic illness, physical needs
IEP
Individualized Education Program
Specialized instruction and related services. Requires qualifying under one of 13 federal disability categories. Includes measurable goals and dedicated service hours.
✦ Best for: learning disabilities, autism, developmental delays, speech/OT needs
