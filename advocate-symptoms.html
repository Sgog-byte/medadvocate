Reference guide for rating your symptoms accurately
Source: NeuraHealth.co ↗
🟢 Mild (1–3)
1 — Hardly notice
Hardly notice the symptom
2 — Notice, no interference
Notice it, does not interfere with activities
3 — Sometimes distracts
Sometimes distracts me
🟡 Moderate (4–6)
4 — Distracts, can function
Distracts me, can do usual activities
5 — Interrupts activities
Interrupts some activities
6 — Hard to ignore
Hard to ignore, avoiding usual activities
🔴 Severe (7–10)
7 — Prevents daily activities
Focus of attention, prevents doing daily activities
8 — Awful, hard to do anything
Awful, hard to do anything
9 — Can't bear it
Can't bear it, unable to do anything
10 — Worst possible
As bad as it could be, nothing else matters
Also consider how symptoms affect:
🏃 Activity — Can you do what you normally do?
😴 Sleep — Does it wake you or prevent sleep?
😔 Mood — Is it affecting how you feel emotionally?
😰 Stress — Is worrying about it adding to your burden?
These four dimensions are included in the full DoD/VA scale and help doctors understand the complete impact of your symptoms — not just the number.
